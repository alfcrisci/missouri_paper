General Seasonal Arima $SARIMA(p,d,q)(P,D,Q)_m$ process $X_t$ is the solution of the following equation

$$\Phi(B^m)\phi(B)\nabla_m^D\nabla^d X_t=\Theta(B^m)\theta(B)Z_t$$

where $Z_t$ is the white noise process. $\nabla_mX_t=X_{t}-X_{t-m}$, $\nabla X_t=X_{t}-X_{t-1}$

Now take your example of $ARIMA(2,1,1)(1,0,0)_7$. This means that \begin{align} \Phi(B^m)&=\Phi(B^7)=1-\Phi_1B^7\\ \phi(B)&=1-\phi_1B-\phi_2B\\ \Theta(B^m)&=\Theta(B^7)=1\\ \theta(B&)=\nabla_7(1+\theta_1B+\theta_2B^2)\\ \end{align} and $B^nX_t=X_{t-n}$.

So in your case prediction $X_t$ must satisfy the equation and $\beta$ vector of regressors, fitted by Arima() function of R forecast package that point to the autoregressive part of predicitive equation: \begin{align} \beta (1-\Phi_1B^7)(1-\phi_1B-\phi_2B)\nabla_5^1X_t=(1+\theta_1B+\theta_2B^2)Z_t \end{align}
